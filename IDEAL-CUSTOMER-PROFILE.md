## Company Overview

**checkmate.tech** automates reference checks and background checks for employers across Australia and New Zealand, with global coverage across 190 countries. Core differentiators are speed of turnaround, a modern UX/UI (both for hiring teams and candidates), better candidate experience, and deep integrations with leading ATS/HRIS platforms.

Primary markets: Australia and New Zealand
Secondary market: Global enterprises with multinational hiring operations

## Ideal Customer Profile

### Firmographics

| Attribute | Ideal Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Company size | 500–2,000 employees | Sweet spot. Below 500 = too small / low volume. Above 2,000 = enterprise procurement complexity. |
| Annual hiring volume | 50–500+ hires/year | Higher volume = stronger ROI case for automation |
| Deal size | $10,000–$150,000 ACV | Occasionally $150k–$3M for large enterprise or global deals |
| Geography | Australia and New Zealand (primary) | Secondary: global companies with ANZ operations |
| Company stage | Established / growth-stage | Not pre-revenue startups; need stable hiring volume |
| Locations | Multi-site or distributed workforce | Drives need for consistent, automated checking process |

### Target Industries (Priority Order)

1. **Healthcare & aged care** — High compliance pressure, mandatory checks (police, AHPRA, working with children), regulated by state and federal bodies. Urgency is built-in.
2. **Financial services & banking** — APRA/ASIC obligations, fraud sensitivity, credit and licence verification requirements. Risk-averse buyers who understand the cost of a bad hire. Critical that anyone handling money has appropriate financial checks.
3. **Government & public sector** — Mandatory vetting requirements, long cycles but large contracts, high compliance maturity.
4. **Retail** — High candidate volume, high turnover, significant fraud and theft risk. Many retail organisations currently do zero background checking despite large, fast-moving workforces. Strong ROI case around risk reduction at scale.
5. **Large enterprises (cross-industry)** — 500+ headcount, structured HR function, formal procurement. Likely replacing a manual process or an underperforming incumbent.
6. **Any high-turnover / high-volume hiring industry** — Logistics, hospitality, construction, labour hire, facilities management. The common thread: large numbers of candidates funnelling through quickly, elevated fraud and qualification risk, and typically little or no formal vetting in place.
7. **Staffing & recruitment agencies** — Secondary segment. High volume but price-sensitive; value speed and candidate experience most.

### Disqualifying Signals (Do Not Prioritise)

- Fewer than 100 employees and fewer than 20 hires per year
- Industries with no compliance or vetting requirements
- Companies with no dedicated HR or People function
- Signed with a competitor in the last 6 months (unless there are strong dissatisfaction signals)
- Purely offshore operations with no ANZ presence

## Buyer Personas

### Primary Champion: HR Manager / People & Culture Manager / Talent Acquisition Manager

**Who they are:** The day-to-day owner of the hiring process. This includes HR Managers, People & Culture Managers, and Talent Acquisition Managers — anyone responsible for moving candidates through the hiring funnel. Usually the first person to feel the pain of slow, manual checks. They initiate the evaluation and internally champion the switch.

**Their priorities:**
- Faster time-to-hire (checks are often a bottleneck)
- Less admin burden on their team
- A better experience for candidates (reduces drop-off)
- Not getting blamed when something goes wrong with a hire

**Pain they feel:**
- Chasing referees manually via phone/email for days
- Inconsistent check quality across the team
- Candidates abandoning the process because it's clunky
- Audit requests they can't easily respond to

**Personalization hooks to look for:**
- LinkedIn posts about hiring challenges, candidate experience, or compliance
- AHRI membership or conference appearances
- Recently joined the company (new to role = open to changing processes)
- Previous employer used checkmate or a competitor

**What to say:** Lead with time savings and candidate drop-off reduction. Compliance is secondary for this persona — they care about making their day easier and their hiring metrics better.

**What NOT to say:** Don't lead with price or technical integration specs. Don't make it sound like more work to implement.

**Preferred CTA:** Short demo (20–30 min), or a free trial / pilot offer on a live requisition.

---

### Secondary Stakeholder: Legal / Risk / Compliance

**Involved in:** Deals above ~$50k, healthcare, finance, and government sectors

**Their priorities:**
- Audit trail and defensible documentation
- Regulatory compliance (Privacy Act, Fair Work, state-based licensing)
- Data security and sovereignty (where is candidate data stored?)
- Liability protection if a hire goes wrong

**What to say:** Emphasise audit trails, data residency in ANZ, Privacy Act compliance, and how checkmate reduces organisational risk exposure.

---

### Secondary Stakeholder: Procurement / IT

**Involved in:** Deals above ~$75k, enterprise and government

**Their priorities (Procurement):** Vendor due diligence, contract terms, pricing structure, references from similar organisations
**Their priorities (IT):** Security review, SSO/SAML, data handling, API/integration documentation

**What to say to Procurement:** Case studies from peer organisations, clear pricing structure, references available on request.
**What to say to IT:** Integration documentation, SOC 2 / ISO certifications, existing ATS/HRIS integration list.

---

### Senior Leadership (CEO / COO / CFO)

**Involved in:** Deals above ~$100k or when a significant incident has occurred (bad hire, compliance failure)

**Their priorities:**
- Risk reduction at an organisational level
- Cost efficiency (ROI on automation vs. manual process)
- Protecting employer brand

**What to say:** Frame as risk infrastructure, not HR software. Reference cost of a bad hire (typically 1–3x salary). Mention peer companies that have made the switch.

## What checkmate Delivers

### Check Types (Full Suite)

checkmate is not just a reference checking tool — it's a comprehensive screening and ongoing compliance platform. Agents should understand the full breadth when assessing fit and building outreach.

| Category | Checks Available |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Reference checks** | Automated reference checking with structured questionnaires |
| **Criminal & police** | National police checks, international criminal checks |
| **Identity & visa** | Visa and work rights verification, identity verification |
| **Financial** | Credit checks, bankruptcy checks, directorship checks — critical for anyone handling money |
| **Qualifications** | Degree and certification verification, professional membership checks |
| **Employment** | Employment history verification |
| **Licences & specialised** | Driver's licence, forklift licence, high-risk work licences, working with children checks, WWVP |
| **Industry-specific** | AHPRA registration, ASIC checks, other professional body memberships |

**Key message:** A prospect doesn't need to use every check type from day one — but the breadth means checkmate can grow with them as their compliance requirements evolve.

### Ongoing Compliance (Not Just Point-in-Time)

checkmate offers continuous/ongoing compliance monitoring — not just checks at the point of hire. This is a major differentiator for industries where licences, registrations, or credentials can lapse post-hire (healthcare, transport, construction, finance). Agents should surface this when prospects are in regulated industries or have roles with ongoing licence requirements.

**Positioning:** "Most companies check once at hire and never again. checkmate can alert you when a licence expires, a registration lapses, or a check needs refreshing — so you're always compliant, not just on day one."

### Primary Pain Points (ranked by frequency)

1. **No checking at all — the "trusting company" problem** — A large portion of ANZ businesses simply hire people without any background verification. They trust CVs at face value and never verify qualifications, criminal history, or right to work. This is the biggest untapped segment. They don't know what they're exposed to until something goes wrong. Detected by: no visible background check vendor, no HR tech stack, job posts with no mention of checks, small HR team relative to company size.

2. **Manual, slow reference check process** — Coordinators spending hours chasing referees by phone and email. Common in companies without a dedicated tool. Detected by: job posts for "reference check coordinator", HR team complaints on LinkedIn/Glassdoor, no visible background check vendor on integrations page.

3. **Fraud and qualification risk** — Candidates misrepresenting qualifications, employment history, or licences. Especially acute when roles involve handling money, operating machinery, working with vulnerable people, or holding professional registrations. A candidate with a falsified forklift licence or undisclosed criminal history is a serious liability. Detected by: industry (finance, healthcare, logistics, construction), high-volume hiring, no visible verification process.

4. **Bad hire or fraud incident** — A recent hiring mistake that caused financial, reputational, or safety damage. Creates urgency to implement proper vetting. Detected by: news articles, Glassdoor reviews mentioning trust/safety issues, leadership changes post-incident.

5. **Compliance or audit pressure** — Regulatory requirement to demonstrate consistent, documented checking (especially healthcare, aged care, finance, government). Detected by: industry regulation signals, job posts mentioning compliance requirements, APRA/ASIC/AHPRA mentions.

6. **Competitor let them down** — Dissatisfied with Fit2Work, CV Check, Xref, Referoo, or First Advantage. Slow turnaround, poor candidate UX, clunky platform, poor support. Detected by: G2/Capterra reviews, LinkedIn complaints, direct mentions in outreach responses.

7. **Scaling hiring and current process can't keep up** — Rapid headcount growth making manual checks unsustainable. Detected by: high open role count, recent funding, expansion announcements, multiple HR hires.

8. **Word-of-mouth referral** — Peer HR professional recommended checkmate directly. This is the highest-converting trigger and should be referenced explicitly in outreach if a referral connection is known.

### High-Priority Trigger Events (search for these)

| Trigger | Signal to Search For | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------------|----------------|
| Rapid hiring ramp | 10+ open roles, recent funding, expansion news | Creates volume pain immediately |
| Bad hire / fraud news | Industry press, Glassdoor, LinkedIn | Creates urgency and budget availability |
| Regulatory change | New licensing requirements, Fair Work updates | Compliance pressure creates buying window |
| Competitor dissatisfaction | G2/Capterra reviews of Fit2Work, CV Check, Xref, Referoo | Switching intent already exists |
| New Head of People hired | LinkedIn job change alerts | New leaders evaluate and replace tools in first 90 days |

| New ATS/HRIS implementation | Job posts mentioning new HR tech, PageUp/SmartRecruiters rollout | Integration opportunity and system review moment |

### "Do Nothing" Competitor (Most Common)

Many prospects are still running checks manually — phone calls, email, spreadsheets. This is the most common displacement. Key signals: job posts for "reference check coordinator", no vendor mentioned on integrations page, small/no HR tech stack.

**Positioning against manual process:**
- "Your coordinator spends X hours per hire on checks — checkmate reduces that to minutes"
- "Manual processes create inconsistency that won't hold up in an audit"
- "Candidates abandon manual processes at higher rates — hurting your employer brand"

### Positioning Angles by Situation

1. **Speed angle:** "Our average turnaround is [X hours] vs industry average of 3–5 days. For a company hiring at your volume, that compounds significantly across the year."

2. **Candidate experience angle:** "Candidates drop off clunky check processes. A modern, mobile-first experience means fewer lost hires at the final step."

3. **Global coverage angle:** "If you're hiring across multiple countries, you need one platform — not a different vendor per region. We cover 190 countries."

4. **Integration angle:** "We integrate natively with [their ATS — PageUp / SmartRecruiters / JobAdder / Bullhorn / Teamtailor / Humanforce / Workday / SAP], so your team doesn't change how they work."

5. **Risk/compliance angle:** "Every check is documented, timestamped, and audit-ready. If you're ever questioned on a hire, you have a defensible record."

6. **Peer social proof angle:** "A number of [healthcare / financial services / government] organisations in ANZ have moved to checkmate in the last 12 months — happy to connect you with a reference."

## Pricing Philosophy

checkmate is priced at the upper end of the market — not the cheapest, not the most expensive. We don't lead with pricing in outreach or early conversations. The focus is always on value, risk reduction, and outcomes.

- **Never discount proactively.** Pricing flexibility exists for the right fit, but it is never advertised or mentioned unprompted.
- **If pricing comes up early:** Redirect to value. "We're not the cheapest option — we're the best option. Let me show you why that matters for a business like yours."
- **If pricing is a stated objection:** Explore whether it's genuine or a proxy for another concern. Often it means they're not yet convinced of the value, not that the price is actually wrong.
- **ROI framing:** The cost of one bad hire — in lost productivity, legal exposure, rehiring costs, and reputational damage — typically dwarfs a year of checkmate fees. This is the reframe to use.

## Buying Process & Sales Cycle

### Typical Sales Cycle: 1–4 months

| Stage | What Happens | checkmate Action |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Awareness | HR Manager discovers checkmate via peer referral, LinkedIn, or outbound | Warm, consultative first touch — not a hard pitch |
| Evaluation | HR Manager does internal research, may trial the platform | Demo focused on their specific use case and ATS integration |
| Internal approval | Legal, Procurement, IT may review (deals $50k+) | Provide security docs, case studies, pricing options |
| Decision | Procurement negotiates, legal reviews contract | Smooth contract process, references available |
| Onboarding | Implementation and ATS integration | Fast setup, local support team |

### Why Deals Stall (Primary Reason: Slow Internal Decision-Making)

The most common reason good-fit prospects don't buy is not objection to checkmate — it's internal inertia. The HR Manager is convinced but struggles to get sign-off internally.

**How to address this in outreach and follow-up:**
- Give the HR Manager language and assets to sell internally (one-page ROI summary, security FAQ, peer references)
- Map multiple stakeholders early — don't rely on a single champion
- Create mild urgency where genuine (implementation timelines, pricing validity, peer company timing)
- Offer a pilot/proof of concept to lower the internal approval bar
- Ask directly: "What would make it easier for you to get this approved internally?"

## Channel & Outreach Strategy

### Primary Channel: LinkedIn (warm) + Email

Most effective outreach combines:
1. LinkedIn engagement first (comment on their posts, connect with a personal note)
2. Follow-up email or LinkedIn DM referencing the connection

### Word-of-Mouth Amplification

Since 80% of business comes from inbound and word-of-mouth from HR professionals recommending to peers:
- Always ask happy customers for a direct referral or to be listed as a reference
- Reference the peer network explicitly in outreach: "A number of [industry] HR leaders have recommended we connect"
- Target AHRI events, HR tech conferences, and LinkedIn HR communities in ANZ

### Messaging Principles

1. **Risk reduction is the primary message.** checkmate's core value is protecting organisations from the consequences of uninformed hiring — fraud, safety incidents, regulatory exposure, reputational damage. Lead every conversation here before anything else.

2. **The "trusting company" angle.** Many ANZ businesses simply don't check. They trust CVs, skip verification, and hope for the best. The opening message for this segment isn't "switch from X to checkmate" — it's "do you know what you're exposing yourself to right now?" This is an education-first, urgency-creation play.

3. **Match check type to their industry pain.** Healthcare = working with children, AHPRA, police. Finance = credit checks, directorship, bankruptcy. Logistics/construction = licences, high-risk work, driver's licence. Retail = reference + police at volume. Don't pitch the full suite — lead with the 2–3 checks most relevant to their world.

4. **Ongoing compliance is a differentiator worth mentioning.** Most competitors are point-in-time. Checkmate's continuous monitoring angle is especially powerful for healthcare, finance, and transport where credentials lapse.

5. **Be specific to their industry.** Generic outreach gets ignored. An aged care provider should hear about AHPRA and working-with-children checks. A retail chain should hear about fraud prevention at scale and high-volume automation.

6. **Keep it short.** HR Managers and Talent Acquisition Managers are busy. First outreach should be under 100 words. The goal is a conversation, not a sale.

7. **Use peer social proof.** "We work with a number of [industry] organisations similar to yours in ANZ" is powerful, especially when word-of-mouth is the dominant growth channel.

8. **Never lead with price.** Redirect pricing questions to value and risk. The cost of a bad hire always exceeds the cost of checkmate.

9. **Make the CTA low friction.** "Worth a 20-min call to see if it's relevant?" not "Book a full demo."

| "We don't really do background checks" | "You're not alone — a lot of ANZ businesses trust the CV and move on. The question is what happens when that trust is misplaced. Happy to walk you through what exposure looks like for a business like yours." |
| "We don't have budget right now" | "Totally understand. The way most teams think about it — the cost of one bad hire, rehire, or compliance incident typically far exceeds a year of checkmate. Worth understanding the numbers before your next budget cycle?" |
| "It's too expensive" | "We're not the cheapest option — and that's intentional. We focus on being the best. What's driving the concern — is it the overall cost, or is it that the value isn't clear yet?" |
| "We're not the right person / need to check with others" | "Totally — who else would be involved? Happy to prepare something for them too." |
| "We're concerned about data security" | "We store all data in Australia, are compliant with the Privacy Act, and have security documentation ready for your IT team." |

## Scoring Calibration for Agents

- 500–2,000 employees
- Healthcare, financial services, government, retail, logistics, or large enterprise
- High-turnover or high-volume hiring industry (retail, hospitality, construction, labour hire)
- Hiring 50+ people per year (inferred from open roles or company size)
- ANZ headquarters or significant ANZ operations
- Uses one of checkmate's integrated ATS/HRIS platforms
- Roles that require licences, registrations, or credentials (drivers, forklifts, healthcare, finance)
- Handles money, vulnerable people, or high-risk environments

### Opportunity Quality — High Score Signals (7+)
- No visible background check vendor (strong "trusting company" displacement signal)
- Evidence of manual reference check process
- Recent rapid hiring (10+ open roles, recent funding/expansion)
- Using an underperforming competitor (Fit2Work, CV Check, Xref, Referoo)
- New Head of People, Talent Acquisition Manager, or HR Director in role within last 6 months
- Healthcare/finance/government/retail industry (built-in compliance or fraud risk)
- Roles requiring licence or credential verification (driver, forklift, healthcare, finance)
- Recent bad hire, fraud, or compliance incident in their industry or company

### Contact Access — High Score Signals (7+)
- HR Manager, People & Culture Manager, or Talent Acquisition Manager identified with active LinkedIn profile
- Content creator (posts about HR, hiring, people culture, compliance)
- Member of AHRI or visible in ANZ HR communities
- Mutual connection in HR network
- Previously worked at a checkmate customer

### Competitive Position — High Score Signals (7+)
- No current background check vendor (manual or nothing) — highest opportunity
- Currently using a known weak competitor (Fit2Work, CV Check, Referoo, Xref)
- ATS/HRIS that checkmate integrates with natively
- Evidence of competitor dissatisfaction (reviews, LinkedIn posts, support complaints)

### Outreach Readiness — High Score Signals (7+)
- Strong personalization anchor (content, peer connection, trigger event)
- Warm path available (peer referral, mutual connection, AHRI community)
